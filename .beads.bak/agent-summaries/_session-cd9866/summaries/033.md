# Task Summary: ant-farm-033

## Task
Installation guide omits pre-commit PII scrub hook documentation.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A — Integrate into existing Step 1 bullet list
Add mention of the pre-commit hook as additional bullet points under the existing Step 1 description. Pros: minimal diff. Cons: Step 1 is titled "Install the Pre-Push Hook" which would be misleading; the pre-commit hook's purpose and behavior are too distinct to reduce to bullets.

### Approach B — Add a "Step 1b" subsection
Insert a new `### Step 1b: Pre-Commit PII Scrub Hook` subsection between Step 1 and Step 2. Pros: dedicated space without renumbering. Cons: "Step 1b" is an awkward numbering convention and implies the pre-commit hook is a secondary concern when it's equally important.

### Approach C — New top-level "Installed Hooks" H2 section
Add an H2 section before Installation with a reference table of both hooks. Pros: clear side-by-side comparison. Cons: separates conceptual reference from the procedural installation steps readers are following; can cause confusion about where to go to actually install.

### Approach D — Rename Step 1 and add hook-specific subsections (SELECTED)
Rename "Step 1: Install the Pre-Push Hook" to "Step 1: Install the Hooks" and add `#### Pre-Push Hook` and `#### Pre-Commit Hook` subsections with purpose, behavior, and install-effect bullets for each. Pros: accurately reflects what `install-hooks.sh` actually does; subsections are scannable and parallel in structure; natural place for per-hook verification steps. Cons: slightly larger diff than Approaches A or B, but the added clarity is worth it.

## Selected Approach

**Approach D** was chosen because renaming Step 1 to "Install the Hooks" is the only accurate description of the script's behavior (it installs two hooks). The parallel `####` subsection structure makes it immediately clear both hooks are equals, enables each to carry its own purpose/behavior/effect documentation, and leaves room for additional hooks in the future without restructuring.
