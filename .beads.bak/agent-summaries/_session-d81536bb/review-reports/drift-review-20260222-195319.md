### Finding 1: GLOSSARY checkpoint count is stale (5 instead of 6)

- **File(s)**: orchestration/GLOSSARY.md:L46, orchestration/GLOSSARY.md:L56, orchestration/GLOSSARY.md:L67
- **Severity**: P2
- **Category**: drift
- **Description**: Three locations in GLOSSARY.md hard-code "five checkpoints" and list only SSV, CCO, WWD, DMVDC, CCB. ESV was added as a sixth hard gate in these commits. Consumers reading the GLOSSARY will be told the system has 5 checkpoints, contradicting checkpoints.md and RULES.md which define 6.
  - L46: "There are five checkpoints: SSV, CCO, WWD, DMVDC, and CCB."
  - L56: "All five checkpoints (SSV, CCO, WWD, DMVDC, CCB) are hard gates."
  - L67: "All five checkpoints are executed by Pest Control."
- **Suggested fix**: Update all three lines to "six" and append "ESV" to each list. E.g. L46: "There are six checkpoints: SSV, CCO, WWD, DMVDC, CCB, and ESV."

### Finding 8: RULES.md Step 3c defer instruction uses stale CHANGELOG phrasing

- **File(s)**: orchestration/RULES.md:L287
- **Severity**: P2
- **Category**: drift
- **Description**: Step 3c says `- **If "defer"**: P1/P2 beads stay open; document in CHANGELOG; proceed to Step 4`. The phrase "document in CHANGELOG" implies the Queen writes directly to CHANGELOG — the old behavior. Since these commits, the Scribe owns CHANGELOG (Step 5b). The corresponding line in reviews.md:L945 was correctly updated to "document deferred items for the Scribe (Step 5b CHANGELOG)" but RULES.md was not synchronized. A Queen following RULES.md Step 3c literally may attempt to write CHANGELOG directly at the defer step, bypassing the Scribe.
- **Suggested fix**: Update RULES.md:L287 to: `- **If "defer"**: P1/P2 beads stay open; document deferred items for the Scribe (Step 5b CHANGELOG); proceed to Step 4`
