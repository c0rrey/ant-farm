## Critical Assessment

### Why Check 1 Failure is Blocking

The briefing states:

> **Rationale**: This is recommended because it produces 0 same-file conflicts for 28 of 30 tasks. Only 2 cross-cutting tasks (a1rf, w2i1) have potential rebase needs, and both involve small localized changes in the secondary files.
>

However, this assessment conflicts with SSV Check 1 constraints. In the SSV framework:

- **File overlap within a wave** = two agents editing the same file in parallel = potential merge conflict
- **"Rebase needs"** does not eliminate the overlap — it acknowledges that merge conflict resolution will be required
- SSV Check 1 has zero tolerance for same-file conflicts, regardless of how "small" the changes are

The Scout's briefing acknowledges the problem but proposes a workaround (rebasing). The Queen must decide whether to:

1. **Accept Strategy A and manually manage rebases** (violates SSV constraint but briefing notes it as acceptable)
2. **Adopt Strategy B** (2 waves, zero conflict risk, longer elapsed time)
3. **Request Scout revision** with a hybrid approach that eliminates the overlaps in Wave 1

### Recommendation to Scout

1. **Adopt Strategy B (Recommended by Scout as fallback)**:
   - Wave 1: Agents 1-7 without cross-cutting tasks (30 agents total)
