## Findings Catalog

### Finding 1: Pantry Section 2 (Review Mode) still documents sleep-based CCB wait protocol

- **File(s)**: `orchestration/templates/pantry.md:236–423`
- **Severity**: P3
- **Category**: drift
- **Description**: The entire Section 2 of pantry.md is marked `[DEPRECATED]` and says it is superseded by `build-review-prompts.sh`. The deprecated block (lines 236–423) contains the old Pantry review-mode workflow that includes a timeout/wait design (Step 0a polling loop, `{{REVIEW_ROUND}}` substitution, 30-second polling). The new turn-based protocol that replaced sleep-based waiting (ant-farm-q59z) lives in `reviews.md` and `big-head-skeleton.md`. No corresponding change was made inside the deprecated section of pantry.md because the section is deprecated — but the deprecated block documents a protocol that is now doubly stale: first superseded by `build-review-prompts.sh`, then the wait protocol inside it was replaced by the turn-based approach. If a future developer reads the deprecated section for reference, the timeout values and sleep-based design will conflict with the current authoritative protocol in reviews.md. The conflict is cosmetic (the section is marked DEPRECATED) but could mislead.
- **Suggested fix**: Update or remove the deprecated Section 2 body, or add a note directly above the Step 4 and Step 0a polling content that the timeout protocol was also replaced by ant-farm-q59z.
- **Cross-reference**: None — this is a stale-doc drift issue, not a logic or naming issue.

### Finding 2: Post-push sync check added to RULES.md Step 6 may overlap with pre-push hook — relationship undocumented

- **File(s)**: `orchestration/RULES.md:365–371`
- **Severity**: P3
- **Category**: drift
- **Description**: Step 6 of RULES.md now includes a post-push `diff -rq` sync check that calls `scripts/sync-to-claude.sh` if the directories diverge. The pre-push hook installed by `scripts/install-hooks.sh` already runs `sync-to-claude.sh` automatically before every `git push`. The two sync mechanisms are not contradictory (the post-push check is a belt-and-suspenders guard for the case where the hook fails silently), but RULES.md does not mention that the hook already handles this on every push. A reader of RULES.md who doesn't know about the hook will believe this is the sole sync mechanism and might be surprised if the hook runs first. Conversely, a developer who relies on the hook may skip the post-push diff check step, missing cases where the hook itself failed.

  The pre-push hook is non-fatal by design (installs it with `if ! "$SYNC_SCRIPT"; then echo WARNING...` — push continues even on sync failure). So the post-push check in RULES.md does add real value as a detection mechanism. But the relationship is undocumented and the two mechanisms describe different behaviors: the hook runs silently and non-fatally before every push; the RULES.md step is an explicit post-push verification with a diff command that surfaces the mismatch. A developer following RULES.md verbatim does not know the hook is also running.
- **Suggested fix**: Add a one-line note in the RULES.md Step 6 post-push sync block explaining the relationship to the pre-push hook, e.g.: "Note: the pre-push hook also runs `sync-to-claude.sh` automatically, but it is non-fatal. This post-push check detects cases where the hook ran but sync failed silently."
- **Cross-reference**: None.

### Finding 3: RULES.md Step 1b progress log field name `tasks_accepted` — parse-progress-log.sh does not read or validate this field

- **File(s)**: `orchestration/RULES.md:116–117`
- **Severity**: P3
- **Category**: drift
- **Description**: The commit (ant-farm-m4si) renamed the `tasks_approved` field to `tasks_accepted` in the RULES.md Step 1b progress log template. The `parse-progress-log.sh` script parses progress.log lines by step_key (second pipe-delimited field) and stores the remaining fields as opaque `details`. It does not inspect or validate field names within the details string. So the rename from `tasks_approved` to `tasks_accepted` has no functional impact on `parse-progress-log.sh`. No stale reference exists in any script. This is confirmed by searching: no occurrence of `tasks_approved` appears anywhere in the codebase after the fix. Finding 3 is noted here for completeness but represents zero drift risk.
- **Suggested fix**: No action needed. The rename is fully propagated.
- **Cross-reference**: None.
